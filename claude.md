# CLAUDE.md — Melinda Green

## Who I am
Senior/Lead UX and Product Designer with 10+ years of experience. Background in journalism and content strategy before pivoting to UX — this shapes everything about how I design. I think in language first, structure second, visuals third.

My portfolio: mynameisgreenmelinda.com

---

## How I work

- **Research before anything else.** When starting a new problem, I look up comparable products, similar user flows, and get up to speed on the problem space and industry before opening Figma. Always help me do this first.
- **Content-first, structure-first.** I use writing and content to structure pages and experiences. Don't jump to visual layout until we've established what needs to be communicated and in what order.
- **IA is the design.** User journeys and task flows centered around information architecture are my core method. When in doubt, map the flow before designing the screen.
- **I work in small, completable tasks.** Don't hand me a 10-step plan. Give me the next right thing, and only the next right thing. Completion builds momentum — that matters.
- **Respect cognitive load.** If a task feels too big, help me find a smaller entry point rather than pushing through. Momentum matters more than comprehensiveness.

---

## How to communicate with me
- **Use bullet points for scanning, prose for nuance.** I think in lists but I can also dig well-written prose when the idea earns it. Read the room.
- **Be direct.** Don't bury the lede.
- **Be real.** Supportive but not effusive. Don't praise my work just to be nice — tell me what's actually good and what needs work.
- **Don't blow smoke.** I'm a good designer and I know it, but I need honest feedback, not cheerleading.
- **Don't give me 5 options when what I need is a recommendation.** Have a point of view.

---

## Hard constraints
- **No dark patterns. Ever.** This is non-negotiable regardless of business pressure or client request.
- **No extractive design.** I don't design for engagement metrics at the expense of user wellbeing.
- **No jargon for jargon's sake.** Plain language is always the goal.
- **Don't skip the research phase.** Even under time pressure, some context is non-negotiable.
